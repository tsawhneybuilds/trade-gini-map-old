Growth effect

Per-Capita Income Growth and Concentration

This page reports descriptive country-panel tests using the rd2 country sample. The main question is whether lagged real per-capita GDP or GNI growth predicts next-year concentration levels after country and year fixed effects.

Growth effect

Income growth and concentration

Question
Does lagged real per-capita GDP or GNI growth predict lower or higher next-year concentration levels?
Supports yes if
The lagged income-growth coefficient is stable under clustered inference and timing/placebo checks.
Weakens yes if
The coefficient disappears under two-way clustering, is matched by future growth, or depends on one income-level bin.
Current answer
Treat as descriptive panel evidence. The page reports country/year fixed effects, placebo, and lagged-income-level checks before making any substantive claim.
Growth effect pageMain model CSVRobustness CSV

Reading the Test

  • The main model is descriptive: country and year fixed effects absorb time-invariant country differences and global shocks, but time-varying confounding remains possible.
  • Income growth uses World Bank real GDP per capita and real GNI per capita in constant 2015 US dollars. Read the estimates as conditional descriptive associations, not causal effects.
  • The largest absolute lagged income-growth coefficient is Exports Partner Top 5% share (Real GDP per capita), coefficient 0.0998, with country-clustered SE 0.0723.
  • Lagged income-level terciles use World Bank real GDP/GNI per capita in constant 2015 US dollars. The first available low/middle cutoff is $6238 and middle/high cutoff is $33251.
  • The leveling-off check compares high-income and low-income growth slopes using a BH q-value threshold of 0.10. The largest high-minus-low difference is Imports Partner Top 1% share (Real GDP per capita), coefficient -0.4306, BH q-value 0.4001.

Main specification

concentrationc,f,t = β prior pc income growthc,t-1 + θ log pc incomec,t-1 + δ log populationc,t-1 + country FE + year FE + εc,f,t

Unit: country-year-flow. Inference: main table clusters by reporter country. Interpretation: coefficients are associations, not causal effects.
