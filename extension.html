Top-Share Evidence

Ginis are high partly because the top products and partners carry large trade shares. “Top item” is the largest single product or partner in a country’s trade basket; “top five” is the combined share of the five largest products or partners. Partner top shares are higher because countries often trade with a small number of major destinations or sources, while product baskets contain thousands of HS6 items.

Which Top-Five Items Actually Raise Gini?

The frequency tables show what appears often. The leave-one-out charts ask a different question: if the item is removed from a country's latest-year trade basket, how much does the Gini fall?

All-reporter average

This is the broader panel comparison because the contribution is averaged across all latest-year reporters, including countries where the item is not in the top five. Positive values mean removing the item lowers Gini, so the item is concentration-raising.

The largest partner-side signals are USA (USA) for export destinations (0.010) and China (CHN) for import suppliers (0.006). On the product side, Crude petroleum oil (HS 270900) is the largest import contributor (0.005), while Refined petroleum oils (HS 271019) leads exported products (0.002).

Conditional on being top five

This view asks how much the item raises concentration in countries where it actually appears in that country's top five. It is useful for mechanism spotting, but rare items can look large because the average is over fewer reporters.

The conditional partner signals remain large for USA (USA) as an export destination (0.015) and China (CHN) as an import supplier (0.008). The product panels reinforce the lumpy-goods story: oil, gold, aircraft, pharmaceuticals, vehicles, and chips repeatedly show positive contributions where they enter top-five baskets.
