Partner-Gini stability

Partner Gini Is Mostly Stable, With Exceptions

This rd2-only page tests whether Partner Gini moves slowly within countries over time. The answer is yes for the cross-country median and most country-flow series, but not literally for every country.

Partner-Gini stability

Mostly stable, not stable for every country

Question
Is Partner Gini relatively stable over time for rd2 countries?
Supports yes if
Most country-flow series have small fitted 10-year changes and small endpoint changes.
Weakens yes if
Common time trends or many country-specific changes are large enough to reject stability.
Current answer
Mostly supports. In 2000-2024, 85.0% of export country series and 91.7% of import country series stay within a +/-0.02 fitted 10-year change. The common export trend is 0.001 per decade; the common import trend is 0.003 per decade. The claim is not true for every country.
Slope distributionCountry exceptionsStability memo

Reading the Test

  • The defensible statement is Partner Gini is relatively stable for most rd2 countries, not that it is stable for all countries.
  • The main window is 2000-2024; the test excludes the partial 2025 tail. The balanced sensitivity window is 2001-2021.
  • Partner Gini is computed across observed positive trade partners after products are summed into partner totals. HS6 999999 is included in partner totals by convention; aggregate partner partnerCode == 0 is excluded upstream.
  • Common time movement is small relative to persistent country differences. Adding year fixed effects raises R-squared by only 0.009 for exports and 0.006 for imports.
  • The exceptions matter: Armenia imports, United Arab Emirates exports, Kyrgyzstan imports, Egypt exports, and China exports have large endpoint changes in the main window.

Common trend specification

PartnerGinic,t = β trendt + country FE + εc,t

Unit: country-year-flow. Inference: common-trend rows cluster standard errors by reporter country. Practical margin: a country-flow series is called slope-stable when its fitted absolute 10-year Partner-Gini change is at most 0.02.

Partner measure

Products are summed into destination or source country totals before concentration is computed. HS6 999999 is included in partner totals by convention because product identity is not part of this measure.

What the test can say

The result is a descriptive stability check. It can support a claim about slow within-country movement in the modern rd2 panel; it is not a causal model and does not explain why exceptions move.

Historical extension caution

The historical 1900-onward question is harder because expanding partner coverage can mechanically look like falling concentration. Historical graphs need partner counts, coverage ratios, source regimes, boundary flags, residual-mass bounds, and synthetic censoring checks before they become results.
