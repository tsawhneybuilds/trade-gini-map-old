Export Growth and Concentration

This page reports descriptive country-panel tests using the rd2 country sample. The main question is whether lagged real aggregate export growth predicts concentration levels one, five, and ten years later after country and base-year fixed effects.

Growth effect

Export growth and concentration

Question
Does lagged real aggregate export growth predict lower or higher concentration levels 1/5/10 years later?
Supports yes if
The lagged export-growth coefficient is stable across horizons under clustered inference and timing/placebo checks.
Weakens yes if
The coefficient disappears under two-way clustering, is matched by future growth, or depends on one export-level bin.
Current answer
Treat as descriptive panel evidence. The page reports country/base-year fixed effects, common-horizon sample matching, placebo, and lagged-export-level checks before making any substantive claim.

Reading the Test

  • The main model is descriptive: country and base-year fixed effects absorb time-invariant country differences and global base-year shocks, but time-varying confounding remains possible.
  • Horizon means years from the export-growth base year to the concentration outcome year. The regression sample is matched across horizons, so 1-, 5-, and 10-year rows use the same country-flow-base-year support; in this run the common base-year window is 1987-2014.
  • The robustness section now compares this balanced/common-support sample with a broad available-horizon sample. The broad sample uses every row available for each horizon, so differences between the two tables flag possible horizon-specific selection.
  • Export growth uses World Bank goods-and-services exports, while the concentration outcomes are merchandise concentration measures. Read the exposure as an aggregate export-growth environment, not the exact concentration denominator.
  • The largest absolute lagged export-growth coefficient is Exports Partner Top 1% share at horizon 1 years, coefficient 0.0784, with country-clustered SE 0.0573.
  • Lagged export-level terciles use World Bank real exports in constant 2015 US dollars. The first available low/middle cutoff is $26333739049 and middle/high cutoff is $171579326937.
  • The leveling-off check compares high-export-level and low-export-level growth slopes using a BH q-value threshold of 0.10. The largest high-minus-low difference is Exports Partner Top 1% share, coefficient -0.1058, BH q-value 0.6100.

Main specification

concentrationc,f,b+h = β [log real exportsc,b - log real exportsc,b-1] + θ log real exportsc,b + δ log populationc,b + country FE + base-year FE + εc,f,b,h

Unit: country-base-year-flow-horizon. Inference: main table clusters by reporter country. Interpretation: coefficients are associations, not causal effects.
