Import concentration mechanisms

Energy, Intermediates, Suppliers, and Input-Output Linkages

The import exercises split the aggregate fact into components. The story is not one mechanism; energy is sharply concentrated, intermediates are large, and supplier concentration matters in narrower product channels.

Exercise 3

Import bins

Question
Import concentration is driven by energy, capital goods, or key intermediates rather than final consumption goods.
Supports yes if
Those bins are internally concentrated and explain large top-product shares or reduce aggregate Gini when excluded.
Weakens yes if

Current answer
Supports a two-part answer. Energy is the sharpest spike, with median bin Gini 0.884 and top-1 share 54.0%; intermediates are the largest part of the bill, with median import value share 47.7%.

Exercise 11

Input-output linkage

Question
Import concentration is tied to export production chains.
Supports yes if
Concentrated intermediate imports map closely to top export sectors, especially for India and production-chain sectors.
Weakens yes if
Import concentration is mostly unrelated to exports, or mostly final consumption/oil with no clear sector link.
Current answer
Mixed. Top export sectors do have concentrated imported-input baskets: median top-sector input product Gini is 0.762, and India's latest matched ICIO year has 0.826. But the broad claim is weak; a narrower supplier-concentrated intermediate/electronics channel is more plausible.

Exercise 11: Export-Linked Imported Inputs

Top export sectors have concentrated imported-input baskets, but the linkage is descriptive. The median top-sector input product Gini is 0.762. India's latest matched ICIO year is 2022, with weighted top-sector input product Gini 0.826 and matched requirement share 7.6%.

Interpretation: the broad story that all concentration-driving imports are export-linked intermediates is weak. A narrower channel through supplier-concentrated intermediate and electronics inputs remains more plausible.
