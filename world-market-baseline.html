Why Does Export Concentration Remain High in Large Economies?

Large countries export a more diversified basket than small countries, but global product markets are themselves unequal in size. This page separates that global-market benchmark from country-specific specialization using harmonized HS1992/H0 exports over 2000-2024.

Question

Is large-country concentration high because globally large products dominate world trade?

Supports the explanation if
Large economies have low divergence from the rest-of-world basket, and the additive global-market component accounts for a large share of their observed Theil concentration.
Weakens the explanation if
Large economies remain highly concentrated after removing the global-market benchmark, or their concentration is mainly country-specific specialization.
Current answer
The evidence supports the benchmark explanation as an important, but incomplete, component. In the largest population quintile, the global-market component accounts for 45.2% of mean observed Theil, compared with 16.6% in the smallest quintile.
Claim type
This is an accounting decomposition plus descriptive regression evidence. It is not a causal estimate of GDP, population, or world demand.

The accounting test

The two components add exactly to observed fixed-universe Theil.

Constructed measures

Tc,t = Σp sc,p,t log(K sc,p,t)
Tc,t = Σp sc,p,t log(K w-c,p,t) + Σp sc,p,t log(sc,p,t / w-c,p,t)

Unit: reporter-year exports. s is the country product share. w-c is the product share in broad-world exports after subtracting that reporter. K is the eligible harmonized product universe. The first term is the global-market component; the second is nonnegative KL divergence from the rest-of-world basket.

HS6 999999, “Commodities not specified,” is excluded before harmonized product aggregation. The broad-primary sensitivity removes raw agriculture, ores, fuels, forestry, precious metals, and first-stage processing before shares are renormalized.

Interpretation

What the decomposition establishes

A positive global-market component means part of measured concentration reflects the fact that cars, petroleum, electronics, and other products occupy very different shares of world trade. A country can therefore be relatively close to the world basket while still having a high absolute concentration index.

What the size gradient adds

The coefficient on population for specialization KL is -0.5536 (raw p <0.001). A negative value means larger countries deviate less from the rest-of-world basket, conditional on GDP per capita and common year shocks.

What this does not establish

The results do not show that becoming larger causally changes a country's export composition. Product capabilities, geography, institutions, global demand, and income are jointly determined with export patterns.

Validation and limitations

  • All programmed validation checks pass.
  • The main benchmark is broad-world product exports, not a separately constructed global import-demand series. It should be interpreted as global product-trade size.
  • 86 country-years are excluded from the preferred sample because current raw-file contents do not reproduce the published checkpoint; the full-current-raw sensitivity yields nearly identical coefficients.
  • 56 country-years are excluded because the leave-one-out benchmark is materially affected by zero or stale rest-of-world product values.
  • Leave-one-country-out shares prevent a large reporter from mechanically defining its own benchmark. A transparent floor is used only where the reporter is the sole broad-world exporter of a product; the maximum affected country-year export share is reported in the diagnostics.
  • Fixed-universe Theil is the primary measure because the decomposition is exact and includes inactive products. Active Gini and HHI are robustness measures with different estimands.
  • The adversarial review was conducted locally rather than by an independent fresh reviewer agent because delegation was not authorized.
  • The population coefficient for specialization KL remains negative in 100% of country jackknife estimates; the leave-one-country-out range is -0.562 to -0.535.
