Weakens yes if

Exercise 12: Harmonized-HS6 Persistent Expansion Decomposition

This rd2-only headline table now uses harmonized HS6 families as the main product identity. It keeps the Evenett-Venables logic: adjacent two-year base and future windows, a $50,000 constant-2024-dollar activity threshold, and pooled positive-expansion shares as the main contribution measure. HS4 is retained below only as a coarser robustness check.

Headline accounting rule

ExpansionShareg = Σ max(ΔXg,c,t,h,0) / Σ max(ΔXc,t,h,0)

Unit: reporter-country, adjacent 2-year base window, adjacent 2-year future window, harmonized HS6 family. Active product rule: at least $50,000 in constant 2024 USD in both years of the window. Net-growth shares are shown as companion accounting and can exceed 100 percent or turn negative when contractions offset expansion.

  • Persistent new harmonized HS6 families account for 43.6% of pooled positive expansion in 5-year windows.
  • Continuing harmonized HS6 families account for 49.2%; dying or non-persistent future products account for 0.2%.
  • For continuing harmonized HS6 families, new product-specific partners account for 13.7% of product-partner positive expansion within continuing product families.
  • Cell-level combined accounting: net-new products are 36.2%, continuing products to new product-specific partners are 7.9%, and new reporter partners are 1.4%.
  • Validation: 60 rd2 countries, harmonized HS6 families, $50,000 threshold in 2024 USD, and 0 HS6 999999 rows after filtering.

Method Caveat: Harmonization

The HS6 headline follows the harmonization concern raised by Lukaszuk and Torun (2022) and the Harvard Growth Lab conversion-weight project. This run uses our current WCO-family harmonization, not the full LT/HGL optimized weighted conversion. In the validation file, clean non-ambiguous trade value is 78.8%, ambiguous-but-assignable trade value is 21.2%, and unmatched trade value is 0.0%.
