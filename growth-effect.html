Export Growth and Concentration

This page reports descriptive country-panel tests using the rd2 country sample. The main question is whether lagged real aggregate export growth predicts next-year concentration levels after country and year fixed effects.

Growth effect

Export growth and concentration

Question
Does lagged real aggregate export growth predict lower or higher next-year concentration levels?
Supports yes if
The lagged export-growth coefficient is stable under clustered inference and timing/placebo checks.
Weakens yes if
The coefficient disappears under two-way clustering, is matched by future growth, or depends on one export-level bin.
Current answer
Treat as descriptive panel evidence. The page reports country/year fixed effects, placebo, and lagged-export-level checks before making any substantive claim.

Reading the Test

  • The main model is descriptive: country and year fixed effects absorb time-invariant country differences and global shocks, but time-varying confounding remains possible.
  • The largest absolute lagged export-growth coefficient is Exports Partner Top 1% share, coefficient 0.1133, with country-clustered SE 0.0408.
  • Lagged export-level terciles use World Bank real exports in constant 2015 US dollars. The first available low/middle cutoff is $28867988330 and middle/high cutoff is $199415411913.
  • The leveling-off check compares high-export-level and low-export-level growth slopes. The largest high-minus-low difference is Exports Partner Top 1% share, coefficient -0.0969, BH q-value 0.9283.

Main specification

concentrationc,f,t = β prior export growthc,t-1 + θ log real exportsc,t-1 + δ log populationc,t-1 + country FE + year FE + εc,f,t

Unit: country-year-flow. Inference: main table clusters by reporter country. Interpretation: coefficients are associations, not causal effects.
