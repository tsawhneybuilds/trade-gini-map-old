Exercise 12

Where export growth comes from

Question
Export growth may come from persistent entry into new HS4 products, product-specific destination spread for continuing products, or expansion of already-present products.
Supports yes if
Persistent new HS4 products and new product-specific partners account for meaningful shares of pooled positive export expansion.
Weakens yes if
Almost all positive expansion comes from continuing HS4 products sold through existing product-specific partners.
Current answer
The stricter headline uses HS4 products, adjacent 2-year base and future windows, a $50,000 constant-2024-dollar activity threshold, and pooled positive-expansion shares. In 5-year rd2 windows, persistent new HS4 products account for 3.0% of pooled positive expansion, while continuing HS4 products account for 96.4%. In the cell-level product-first view, net-new products account for 2.2% and continuing HS4 products to new product-specific partners account for 8.7%. In the partner-first view, new reporter partners account for 2.1%.

Exercise 12: HS4 Persistent Expansion Decomposition

This rd2-only headline table follows the Evenett-Venables logic more closely than the rolling HS6 cell table: HS4 products, adjacent two-year base and future windows, a $50,000 constant-2024-dollar activity threshold, and pooled positive-expansion shares as the main contribution measure.

Headline accounting rule

ExpansionShareg = Σ max(ΔXg,c,t,h,0) / Σ max(ΔXc,t,h,0)

Unit: reporter-country, adjacent 2-year base window, adjacent 2-year future window, HS4 product. Active product rule: at least $50,000 in constant 2024 USD in both years of the window. Net-growth shares are shown as companion accounting and can exceed 100 percent or turn negative when contractions offset expansion.

  • Persistent new HS4 products account for 3.0% of pooled positive expansion in 5-year windows.
  • Continuing HS4 products account for 96.4%; dying or non-persistent future products account for 0.1%.
  • For continuing HS4 products, new product-specific partners account for 9.0% of product-partner positive expansion within continuing HS4 products.
  • Cell-level combined accounting: net-new products are 2.2%, continuing HS4 products to new product-specific partners are 8.7%, and new reporter partners are 2.1%.
  • Validation: 60 rd2 countries, HS4 product level, $50,000 threshold in 2024 USD, and 0 HS6 999999 rows after filtering.
