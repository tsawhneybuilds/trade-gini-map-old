Exercise 12: LT/HGL HS1992 Persistent Expansion Decomposition

This rd2-only headline table now uses official LT/HGL weighted conversion to HS1992/H0 as the main product identity. It keeps the Evenett-Venables logic: adjacent two-year base and future windows, a $50,000 constant-2024-dollar activity threshold, and pooled positive-expansion shares as the main contribution measure. HS4 is retained below only as a coarser robustness check.

Headline accounting rule

ExpansionShareg = Σ max(ΔXg,c,t,h,0) / Σ max(ΔXc,t,h,0)

Unit: reporter-country, adjacent 2-year base window, adjacent 2-year future window, LT/HGL HS1992 product. Active product rule: at least $50,000 in constant 2024 USD in both years of the window. Net-growth shares are shown as companion accounting and can exceed 100 percent or turn negative when contractions offset expansion.

  • Persistent new LT/HGL HS1992 products account for 4.0% of pooled positive expansion in 5-year windows.
  • Continuing LT/HGL HS1992 products account for 95.3%; dying or non-persistent future products account for 0.1%.
  • For continuing LT/HGL HS1992 products, new product-specific partners account for 13.0% of product-partner positive expansion within continuing product families.
  • Cell-level combined accounting: net-new products are 3.0%, continuing products to new product-specific partners are 12.5%, and new reporter partners are 1.9%.
  • Validation: 60 rd2 countries, LT/HGL harmonized HS6 family, $50,000 threshold in 2024 USD, and 0 HS6 999999 rows after filtering.

Method Caveat: Harmonization

The HS6 headline follows the harmonization concern raised by Lukaszuk and Torun (2022) and uses the Harvard Growth Lab / Dataverse weighted conversion tables to map each source HS6 revision to HS1992/H0 before aggregation. In the validation file, weighted conversion coverage is n/a, unmatched trade value is 0.0%, and the conversion value residual is n/a.
